  • Title of the Project: eDrive Software-in-Loop Simulation (April, 2022 – March, 2026)

    Objective: To reduce vehicle production time and validation costs, a digitalisation strategy was proposed to validate cars in a complete digital simulation environment. To enable it, a Software-in-Loop simulation that includes Virtual ECUs and a virtual vehicle model needs to be developed from scratch. There was no clarity on what to integrate, how to integrate, or prioritisation. No historical data was available to estimate the integration time and challenges from previous similar system-level Software-in-Loop projects.

    Methodology: The end-to-end integration responsibility was assigned to me with a team of 03 members. I created a scope and schedule for each team member and empowered them to integrate, debug, and understand software documents. I used my expertise and knowledge developed in previous projects within the Hardware-in-Loop ecosystem.

    I decided to have the team manually develop the environment and gain experience with best practices and methods, rather than automating from the beginning.

    After the 3 simulation variants were successfully integrated and delivered results to stakeholders, I actively deployed my team to enable automated integration and debugging.

    Challenges: The key challenge was to get the Virtual ECUs from other departments and suppliers. The simulation environment contains 07 different Virtual ECUs. Getting them in working condition and on time was a challenge and imposed risks. I estimated the expected delays from different meetings with them and informed them to take appropriate action.

    Another key challenge was that at the beginning of the project, upon integration of the Virtual ECUs, the system did not work. Since the Virtual ECUs are in binary format, debugging at the system level after integration becomes extremely tough. In the beginning, I guided the team with my domain knowledge to debug at the system level. Later, I decided to use AI and data to identify the root causes at the system level; this further led to the development of a tool.

    Management Principles: I applied Project Management knowledge areas and Value Stream Mapping to identify and control deviations. I empowered the team in integration, coding, CI/CD and debugging, frequently shared updates with stakeholders, applied SCRUM to deliver solutions on time, prioritised backlogs, analysed stakeholder pain points and provided effective solutions, tracked and controlled the project scope and schedule, and enabled automation in integration and debugging.

    Outcome: The simulation setups serve as the basis for many use cases across the organisation. The simulation platforms are released with every software increment for system-level validation. One variant of the simulation setup was used for software validation of the Mercedes-Benz CLA (MMA), launched in March 2025. The concept is being extended for future projects.

    Objective: Conventional automation fails when the environment is non-deterministic. Hence, many automation pipelines failed and needed human intervention. Hence, the leadership team decided to develop the PoC jointly between the Domain Experts from the Software-in-Loop team and the AI team.

    Methodology: I was assigned to the project to direct the team, consisting of 04 members: 02 from the simulation team and 02 from the AI team, to develop the PoC. I interacted with 17 users in the vertical and collected pain points and ideas. I prioritised the pain areas to align with the proposed solution, designed the solution architecture, and defined the scope and schedule. While creating backlogs and architecture and providing requirements to the AI team, I decided to make them generic so we could scale them in future. I arranged daily short meetings to discuss the blockers and effectively eliminated them. After developing the solution, I validated the framework and created the final presentation with results.

    Challenge: The key challenge was from the hallucination of the models. It was eliminated by the team with more rule-based conditions.

    Management Principles: I applied Design Thinking to identify key pain points across the vertical, managed the project's scope and schedule, and eliminated waste through Kaizen.

    Outcome: The team completed the PoC on time. I presented the results to the senior leadership team. The leadership team decides to extend the project into a product.

  • Title of the Product: DeepResolve (January, 2024 – December, 2025)

    Pain Areas Addressed: The Virtual ECUs received from other departments and suppliers are in binary form. Upon integration, the system-level platform fails frequently due to interactions and component-level errors. Since the Virtual ECUs are in binary format and closed-loop interactions, system-level debugging becomes extremely difficult. The error from a single Virtual ECU propagates throughout the system, making it extremely difficult to localise. Hence, system-level debugging needs domain expertise of the entire system and manual effort. Manual debugging is time-consuming, and releases are delayed, affecting stakeholders directly.

    My Role: I conducted Design Thinking to identify pain points across different stages of manual debugging, gathering ideas from the team. I prioritised ideas using the RICE framework and identified features for the tool. I selected one team member to develop the tool with me. I designed a bidirectional LSTM model for pattern recognition to address anomalies. After the MVP was complete, I presented the tool's value in multiple branding forums in India and Germany.

    Value Delivered: DeepResolve helped the team resolve complex problems quickly, which used to take days. Teams in India and Germany use the tool to find complex defects. Achieved a reduction in defect debugging time from 9 days to 1–3 days with the tool. One case involved identifying the root cause of a deviation in a controller's behaviour resulting from changes to the toolchain. The central controller, when converted into a Virtual ECU by a team in Germany, behaves differently from past builds due to the new toolchain. It was a critical issue that impacted all stakeholders. DeepResolve was used to identify the root cause within a few hours. The extended version of the tool was filed for a patent.
