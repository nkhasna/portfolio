    Objective: To reduce vehicle production time and validation costs, a digitalisation strategy was proposed to validate cars in a complete digital simulation environment. No historical data was available to estimate the integration time and challenges from previous similar system-level Software-in-Loop projects.

    Methodology: The end-to-end integration responsibility was assigned to me with a team of 03 members. Since the Virtual ECUs are in binary format, debugging at the system level after integration becomes extremely tough. In the beginning, I guided the team with my domain knowledge to debug at the system level. Later, I decided to use AI and data to identify the root causes at the system level; this further led to the development of a tool.

    Management Principles: I applied Project Management knowledge areas and Value Stream Mapping to identify and control deviations. I empowered the team in integration, coding, CI/CD and debugging, frequently shared updates with stakeholders, applied SCRUM to deliver solutions on time, prioritised backlogs, analysed stakeholder pain points and provided effective solutions, tracked and controlled the project scope and schedule, and enabled automation in integration and debugging.

One variant of the simulation setup was used for software validation of the Mercedes-Benz CLA (MMA), launched in March 2025. The concept is being extended for future projects.

  • Title of the Product: DeepResolve (January, 2024 – December, 2025)

    Pain Areas Addressed: The Virtual ECUs received from other departments and suppliers are in binary form. Upon integration, the system-level platform fails frequently due to interactions and component-level errors. Since the Virtual ECUs are in binary format and closed-loop interactions, system-level debugging becomes extremely difficult. The error from a single Virtual ECU propagates throughout the system, making it extremely difficult to localise. Hence, system-level debugging needs domain expertise of the entire system and manual effort. Manual debugging is time-consuming, and releases are delayed, affecting stakeholders directly.

    My Contributions: I conducted Design Thinking to identify pain points across different stages of manual debugging, gathering ideas from the team. I prioritised ideas using the RICE framework and identified features for the tool. I selected one team member to develop the tool with me. I designed a bidirectional LSTM model for pattern recognition to address anomalies. After the MVP was complete, I presented the tool's value in multiple branding forums in India and Germany.

    Value Delivered: DeepResolve helped the team resolve complex problems quickly, which used to take days. Teams in India and Germany use the tool to find complex defects. Achieved a reduction in defect debugging time from 9 days to 1–3 days with the tool. One case involved identifying the root cause of a deviation in a controller's behaviour resulting from changes to the toolchain. The central controller, when converted into a Virtual ECU by a team in Germany, behaves differently from past builds due to the new toolchain. It was a critical issue that impacted all stakeholders. DeepResolve was used to identify the root cause within a few hours. The extended version of the tool was filed for a patent.
